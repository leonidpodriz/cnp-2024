Conference Scope

The "Cybersecurity and Network Protection" conference is an essential gathering for experts, researchers, and practitioners in the field of digital security. Our scope encompasses a wide range of topics, focusing on the latest trends, challenges, and advancements in cybersecurity and network protection.

Key areas of discussion will include:

  • Emerging Threats and Vulnerabilities: Understanding the evolving landscape of cyber threats and exploring new vulnerabilities in both software and hardware.
  • Innovative Defense Strategies: Showcasing cutting-edge techniques and tools for defending against cyber attacks, including AI and machine learning-based solutions.
  • Network Security: Delving into the intricacies of securing both private and public networks, with a special focus on new protocols and encryption methods.
  • Cybersecurity Policy and Law: Examining the legal and regulatory framework surrounding cybersecurity, and discussing best practices for compliance and governance.
  • Incident Response and Management: Sharing insights on effective strategies for responding to and managing security breaches and cyber incidents.
  • Data Protection and Privacy: Addressing the challenges in safeguarding sensitive data and maintaining user privacy in an increasingly connected world.
  • Future Technologies: Exploring the implications of emerging technologies like quantum computing and blockchain on cybersecurity.

The conference will feature keynote speeches, panel discussions, and technical sessions led by renowned experts. It will also provide a platform for networking, collaboration, and the sharing of ideas to drive forward the field of cybersecurity.

Join us as we delve into these critical topics and work together towards a more secure digital future.

Call for Contribution

We invite academic researchers and practitioners to share their work by giving a talk or presenting a poster in pure English. Contributions should be submitted through EasyChair in two different categories:

  • A short paper (with references) but has between 5-9 CEUR-WS pages.
  • A regular paper has at least 10 CEUR-WS pages and an appropriate number of references.

When designing your сontribution, use the new Word Template in CEURART for Two Column. Papers designed according to other templates will be rejected (and see an example).

When preparing materials, pay attention to the restrictions:

  • One author can submit up to two сontributions.
  • The number of authors in one сontribution should be no more than five.
  • Self-citation should not exceed 30% (of the total number of sources).

Contributions will be reviewed and selected based on relevance and quality. Contributors will have the option to indicate their preference for the format of the presentation, but organizers reserve the right to the final decision in the matter.
